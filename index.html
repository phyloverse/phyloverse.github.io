Nicole Mitidieri-Rivera

About me

Evolutionary botanist from the Global South interested in the systematics, biogeography, and evolutionary history of Neotropical epiphytic, hemiepiphytic, and arboreal plants. In my PhD in Dr. Kenneth Sytsma's team at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, I studied the evolution of floral characters in Moraceae and the systematics, hybridization, and taxonomy of Ficus sect. Americanae using phylogenetic and geometric morphometric approaches.

Past Projects

Nomenclatural review of Ficus sect. Americanae from South America

Continuous revisions in nomenclature are crucial for maximally informative and stable classification systems as well as accurate assessments of biodiversity. As a precursor to a phylogenetic-based, taxonomic monograph of Neotropical Ficus sect. Americanae (Moraceae), a comprehensive nomenclatural index of all published names in the section from South America was published in Phytotaxa.

Character evolution and diversification in Moraceae

If we replayed the tape of life, would large lineages evolve into the same dazzling diversity observed today? We used a dated phylogenomic backbone of Moraceae to investigate diversification patterns and their drivers in Ficus and allied genera using maximum-likelihood and Bayesian methods. A morphological matrix of 508 species in Moraceae was assembled to explore patterns of trait evolution.
